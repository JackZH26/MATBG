# Cover Letter Draft for Physical Review B

Date: June 10, 2026

Dear Editors,

I am pleased to submit the revised manuscript titled "Interband Pairing
Signatures in the Superfluid Response of Magic-Angle Twisted Bilayer Graphene"
for consideration as a Regular Article in Physical Review B.

The manuscript addresses how the orbital structure of a superconducting
pairing ansatz can leave a separable signature in the finite-band superfluid
response of magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene.  The work constructs an
executable Bistritzer-MacDonald--Bogoliubov-de Gennes workflow in which a
layer-sublattice orbital pairing direction is projected into the band basis
using a documented time-reversal-sewn valley convention.  The central result
is a reproducible, normalization-conditioned change in the normalized isotropic
response when the interband component of the orbital pairing direction is
increased.

The scope of the manuscript is deliberately conservative.  The claim-bearing
observable is the normalized response ratio
`D_iso(mu, eta) / D_iso(mu, eta=0) - 1`; the manuscript does not claim a final
absolute stiffness prediction, quantitative agreement with experimental
stiffness, or a device-level carrier-density calibration.  The Supplemental
Material documents the numerical conventions, table provenance, convergence
and filling sufficiency audits, and the excluded interpretations of the
current mechanism-paper scope.  In response to the major-revision report, the
revised manuscript now adds a pairing-family scan, a grid/truncation/shell
robustness matrix, and a response-level valley-partner sensitivity audit.  A
separate point-by-point response to the referee report is included with this
revision package.

I believe the manuscript is suitable for Physical Review B because it connects
multiband pairing structure, quantum-geometric response diagnostics, and
magic-angle graphene superconductivity in a reproducible finite-band
calculation.  The result complements recent experimental and theoretical work
on superfluid stiffness, nodal or unconventional gap structure, and
band-off-diagonal or finite-momentum pairing scenarios in moire graphene.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and has been
prepared by the sole author.
