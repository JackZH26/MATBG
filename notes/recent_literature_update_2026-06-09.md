## Manuscript Impact

The 2025 Nature stiffness measurement strengthens the motivation for treating
superfluid stiffness as a primary observable rather than a secondary inference
from gap or transport data.

The 2025 Nature Communications MATBG dynamics paper and the 2025 twisted
trilayer stiffness paper add independent support for discussing anisotropic or
nodal gap phenomenology, but they do not change the present normalized-response
claim.

The 2025/2026 tunneling and STM papers strengthen the motivation for comparing
gap spectroscopy with stiffness, intervalley order, and finite-momentum
pairing. They should be cited only as motivation and limitation context because
the current calculation does not model tunneling matrix elements, STM tip
effects, intervalley coherent parent states, or a finite-momentum order
parameter.

The 2025 and 2026 Kekule / pair-density-wave preprints are relevant but not
absorbed into the present calculation. They study finite-momentum pairing
scenarios and gapless quasiparticle effects, while the current manuscript uses
a uniform orbital-projected pairing family in a finite-band BdG response engine.

The 2023 Nature Communications and 2025 PRB papers make band-off-diagonal
pairing a direct predecessor of the present work. The manuscript should not
claim to introduce the concept of band-off-diagonal superconductivity. Its
distinct role is narrower: define an orbital-basis pairing direction, project
it through a declared BM valley-sewing convention, and test whether the
resulting interband component leaves a normalization-conditioned signature in
the finite-band superfluid response.

The 2026 dielectric-environment paper supports the conservative statement that
the microscopic pairing mechanism remains unsettled. It should not be used to
claim that the present ansatz identifies the pairing glue.

The Zhou 2026 arXiv entry is the current author's prior band-basis
decomposition/baseline paper. It is useful as provenance for the benchmark
self-audit, not as external evidence for the new interband-pairing mechanism.

## Decision

Add the 2025 direct stiffness measurement, MATBG dynamics measurement, twisted
trilayer stiffness context, and 2026 interaction-environment constraint to the
Introduction. Cite the 2025/2026 finite-momentum and tunneling/STM papers in
the Discussion as limitation/future-extension context. Do not claim that the
current uniform-pairing scan covers Kekule or PDW order.
Add Christos-Sachdev-Scheurer and Putzer-Scheurer to the Introduction so the
paper is positioned as a response-diagnostic complement to existing
band-off-diagonal pairing theory.
